## Research Goals
Identify pain points in remembering what is in one’s fridge and pantry.

## Interview Information
Always thank the interviewee!

We are gathering some information about how you manage things like food and groceries. There are no right or wrong answers, just curious about your experiences.

This interview information will be used to help my team develop a future product to help people manage their groceries within households.

## Interview Questions
### Intro Questions
- What is your favorite type of food?
- Do you cook? If so, how often?
- What is your kitchen or fridge setup like at home?
- What does a typical week look like for you when it comes to cooking or meals?

### Lead-In Questions
- Do you currently share your fridge with anyone? 
  - Possible Follow-up: Do you eat any meals together? Cook together, separately, or a bit of both?
  - Possible Follow-up: How do you determine what is shared?
  - Possible Follow-up: Has there ever been a time you were confused about who owns what?
  - Possible Follow-up: How do you determine who buys what?
  - Possible Follow-up: How are items split cost-wise?
  - Possible Follow-up: How do you feel about sharing food with roommates in general?
- Do you buy a lot of snacks or a lot of ingredients?
  - Ingredient household vs snack household
- Do you have any routines for cleaning or organizing the fridge?

### Base Questions
- When you go grocery shopping, how do you decide what groceries you’re going to buy?
- When you think about what is in your fridge, how do you usually keep track of it?
- Do you use any tools to keep track?
- If you could change one thing about how you manage groceries, what would it be?
  - Possible Follow-up: Do you feel like tracking your inventory is helpful? Or would it just be extra work?
- Do you frequently let food go bad/expire?
  - Possible Follow-up: How do you handle items that expire?
- How often do you buy groceries, and how do you usually plan your grocery list?
- What would success look like for you – less food waste, saving money, cooking more often, etc?

### Product Specific
- Would it be useful if an app reminded you when food is about to expire?
- What features would you like to see in an app that helps you track your groceries?
- How frequently would you expect reminders to be for items that are about to expire?
- Would you be willing to use an app that helps you track your groceries?
  - Possible Follow-up: What would be some reasons that you would forget to use it?
  - Possible Follow-up: How could one be encouraged to continue using said app?
- Would an expense tracker feel helpful or awkward?
- How would you want the app to handle fairness or accountability?
- If this app existed, how would you implement it into your life?
